Our Journey

Chandrayaan-3, the third mission in the Chandrayaan program, successfully launched on July 14, 2023, from the Satish Dhawan Space Centre in Sriharikota, India. The mission's objectives include: Exploring the lunar south pole: This region is of particular interest because it contains water ice deposits, which could be a potential resource for future human missions to the Moon. Probing the lunar interior: The mission will use a combination of instruments to study the Moon's crust, mantle, and core. Investigating the lunar exosphere: The exosphere is the very thin atmosphere surrounding the Moon. The mission will study its composition and how it is affected by solar and cosmic radiation.

Components

Orbiter

The orbiter will map the Moon's surface, study its composition, and monitor its environment The Chandrayaan-3 Orbiter It is designed to carry out a comprehensive range of scientific investigations, including:

  • Mapping the Moon's surface: The Orbiter will use a high-resolution camera to create detailed maps of the Moon's surface. These maps will help scientists to understand the Moon's geology and history
  • Studying the Moon's composition: The Orbiter will use spectrometers to study the composition of the Moon's soil and rocks. This information will help scientists to understand the Moon's formation and evolution.
  • Monitoring the Moon's environment: The Orbiter will monitor the Moon's atmosphere, radiation environment, and dust levels. This information will help scientists to understand the Moon's potential for future human exploration.

    • Lander

    The Chandrayaan-3 Lander is a crucial component of India's third lunar exploration mission. It is designed to touch down in the lunar south pole region and conduct a series of scientific investigations, including:

  • Searching for water ice: The south pole of the Moon is thought to have permanently shadowed regions that could harbor water ice. The Lander will use a drill to collect samples of regolith from these regions and analyze them for the presence of water ice.
  • Studying the lunar regolith: The regolith is the loose, powdery material that covers the Moon's surface. The Lander will collect samples of the regolith and study its composition and properties.
  • Investigating the lunar environment: The Lander will monitor the lunar environment, including the temperature, pressure, and radiation levels. This information will help scientists to understand the Moon's potential for future human exploration.

    • Lander's Role in Chandrayaan-3 Mission

    The Lander will play a critical role in the Chandrayaan-3 mission by:

  • Landing safely on the Moon's surface: The Lander will use a sophisticated landing system to touch down in the lunar south pole region.
  • Conducting a series of scientific investigations: The Lander will use its instruments to conduct a variety of scientific experiments.
  • Collecting and analyzing regolith samples: The Lander will collect regolith samples and analyze them for the presence of water ice.

  • Relaying data back to Earth: The Lander will relay data back to Earth via the Orbiter.

    The Chandrayaan-3 Lander is expected to make significant scientific contributions, including:

  • Discovery of water ice in the lunar south pole
  • Improved understanding of the lunar regolith
  • New insights into the lunar environment

    Rover's Role in Chandrayaan-3 Mission

    The Rover will play a critical role in the Chandrayaan-3 mission by:

  • Traversing the lunar surface The Rover will use its six wheels to traverse the lunar surface, collecting data and samples along the way
  • Conducting a series of scientific investigations: The Rover will use its instruments to conduct a variety of scientific experiments.
  • Collecting and analyzing regolith samples: The Rover will collect regolith samples and send them to the Lander for analysis

    • November 29, 2023

    The Chandrayaan-3 orbiter is still operating and providing valuable scientific data about the Moon. The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) is continuing to investigate the cause of the communication loss with the Vikram lander.

    The journey of Chandrayaan-3 has been a remarkable one, and it has demonstrated India's continued commitment to lunar exploration. The loss of communication with the Vikram lander is a setback, but the mission has still achieved several significant milestones. The ISRO is determined to learn from this experience and continue its exploration of the Moon.
